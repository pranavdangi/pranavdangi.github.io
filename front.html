Research:
My research spans computer architecture, VLSI design/integration, circuits and compilers.
Specifically, I focus on execution paradigms for reconfigurable dataflow architectures—well, CGRAs—and their programming abstractions.
Lately, my work has tried to tackle sparse and irregular applications that hit the memory wall.

Previously:
I worked on the firmware for RTEMS (Real Time Executive for Multiprocessor Systems) as a Google Summer Of Code Fellow,
had a brief stint working on physical design for the Interconnects IP Group at Intel,
worked on performance modeling and profile-guided scale-out system design with the High-Performance Computing group at TCS Research,
and I was involved in computer arithmetic and VLSI design research during my undergrad at BITS Pilani.
