Research:
I am currently advised by Prof. Tulika Mitra.
My research spans computer architecture, VLSI design/integration, circuits and compilers.
More specifically, I currently work on execution paradigms for reconfigurable dataflow architectures (well, CGRAs) and their programming abstractions.
Oflate, my work has tried to tackle sparse and irregular applications that hit the memory wall.

Previously:
I was a Google Summer of Code Fellow with RTEMS (Real Time Executive for Multiprocessor Systems),
had a short stint with the Physical Design team for the Interconnects IP Group at Intel,
I have worked with TCS Research's High Performance Computing group in Mumbai,
and was invloved in Computer Arithmetic and VLSI design research during my undergrad at BITS Pilani.
